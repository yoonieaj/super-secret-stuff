A closer look at the Michelin Guide

What is the Michelin Guide?

The Michelin Guide's restaurant awards are considered among the most prestigious in the industry. It currently recognises restaurants in 52 countries (as of 2026) and continues to expand. According to Michelin, the guide aims to "cover restaurants across the globe" and does not have a preference for formal, expensive restaurants or specific cuisines (Source). What do these awards mean?

One, Two, and Three star awards are the most prestigious, with more stars being better. While Michelin's stance is that a restaurant does not need to be formal or expensive to receive a star, the price breakdowns for starred restaurants suggest a different story.

The Bib Gourmand award is described as "a special distinction recognizing restaurants that serve high-quality food at great value" (source). This award is reserved for restaurants where the food is not expensive. Try taking a look at the price breakdowns for these restaurants!

Selected restaurants are restaurants where "the inspectors have found the food to be above average, but not quite at star or Bib level" (source).

What are "Regional keywords"?

Michelin tracks the cuisine style of each restaurant that receives an award. Some of these cuisines contain keywords correspond to regional cooking styles (e.g., Classic French, Japanese Contemporary), while others do not (e.g., Seafood, Modern Cuisine). The top regional keywords of a country's awarded restaurants are the most common regional keywords that appear in its awarded restaurants' cuisine categories.
